Straight answers

Questions firms actually ask.

"We don't really use AI."
Almost every firm says that, and almost every firm is wrong. The tools came in through your staff, not your strategy — someone's running listing copy through ChatGPT, someone pasted a client file in to summarize it. That's precisely why the Snapshot exists: to show you what's actually running before it becomes a problem.
"Isn't this just hiring a consultant?"
A consultant hands you a deck and leaves. A fractional CAIO owns the outcome on an ongoing basis — the policy, the tool decisions, the guardrails — and is accountable as your AI use evolves. The difference that matters here: I know the regulatory surface you operate under. Most AI consultants don't, and that's exactly where the liability hides.
"Can't our IT person or software vendor handle this?"
IT can secure a network; a vendor will sell you their tool. Neither one is going to tell you that an AI-generated description reads as steering under fair housing, or that AI-assisted comp commentary compromises appraisal independence. This isn't an IT problem — it's a compliance-and-judgment problem that happens to involve software.
"We're too small for this."
Smaller firms are often more exposed, not less — no compliance department, no policy, and the owner is personally on the hook. The fractional model exists for exactly this: you get senior oversight scaled to your size, not a full-time hire you can't justify.
"Are you a lawyer? Is this legal advice?"
No, and no. I'm a California-certified appraiser and licensed broker who has operated inside these rules for 20+ years. I help you build governance that respects fair housing, USPAP, RSO/AB 1482, and your data-licensing terms — and I'll tell you when something genuinely needs your counsel. Think of me as the person who knows where the tripwires are and makes sure your AI adoption doesn't hit them.
"Will you try to sell us AI tools?"
No. I'm independent of any vendor. My job is to help you choose and govern tools well, which sometimes means telling you to drop one. No referral fees, no kickbacks — that independence is the point.
"What does it cost?"
Far less than the full-time CAIO the work would otherwise require, and a fraction of what a single bad outcome — a fair-housing complaint, a data breach, a compromised valuation — would cost you. Engagements are scoped to the firm, so the honest answer is we size it on the Snapshot. The Snapshot itself is free.
